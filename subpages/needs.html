Needs


‎ 1. First, we would need to plan out the information, media, and the script that our group will use in order to create our
‎ educational documentary. And keeping our audiences’ interest in mind, we will have to craft an engaging storyline and video,
‎ while also making sure that the information we’ll use throughout the educational documentary is correct, true, and focuses on
‎ our main point, which is to spread awareness about the problems of Sitio Bebe and to promote our fundraising concert.

‎ 2. In addition to interviews, we can include footage that captures the daily activities of the community, showcasing both the
‎ hardships and the resilience of its members. This visual storytelling will help to engage the audience on an emotional level,
‎ encouraging them to empathize with the people of Sitio Bebe. Ultimately, our goal is to raise awareness about their situation and
‎ inspire viewers to take action, whether through donations, volunteering, or advocating for support for the community.

‎ 3. The audience requires content that builds a strong emotional bond through impactful storytelling, provides transparency by
‎ demonstrating how funds are utilized, promotes active involvement with interactive updates, and features a clear, compelling
‎ call-to-action to inspire immediate donations.

‎ 4. Finally end the video with clear instructions on how to purchase tickets (e.g., website, phone number, QR codes).
‎ Include early bird offers or group discounts to encourage quick action. People’s attention spans are short, especially on
‎ social media. Short videos that are 15–30 seconds can quickly engage potential attendees. Video content is more engaging
‎ and more likely to be shared compared to text-based posts. Short videos with a compelling call-to-action can lead directly to
‎ ticket purchases, increasing the number of attendees at the concert.
